About the Artist

Painting as a Path to Wholeness

Color, for Yehudis, is not decoration — it is a medium of the soul. Her training in Art Therapy has given her the understanding that art carries a healing intelligence of its own. This understanding informs every stroke, every choice of pigment, every composition.

What Yehudis brings to the canvas is the language from the interiors of holy. Rooted in her Jewish faith and an intimate relationship with the sacred, she does not paint what she sees but what she remembers — or what she hopes to remember.

Her studio is in Jerusalem, Israel, where she works on large canvases in a light that she describes as the most honest light she has ever painted by.

Artistic Philosophy

Where Color Becomes Feeling

"I paint to give form to what lives inside — to make visible the light and shadow that words alone cannot carry."

Yehudis works primarily in oil on large canvas, building layers of color that breathe and move like living light. Her palette comes from intuition and spiritual attentiveness — colors chosen not for their visual logic but for their emotional truth.

Her subjects — ancestors, sacred mountains, prophets, blooming — are not illustrations of ideas but encounters. Each painting is an act of presence, a form of prayer made visible.

With training in Art Therapy, she sees creation not only as personal expression but as a communal act. A painting on your wall continues to do its work long after it leaves the studio.
